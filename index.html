Abstract

As an important means of target observation, Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) are widely used in target recognition. In high-frequency region SAR images, the scattering centers are localized that characterize the physical properties of the target. The Attributed Scattering Center (ASC) models the two-dimensional scattering, but it has high parameter estimation complexity due to its high-dimensional parameter space and nonlinearity. Using the locality and sparsity of target scattering centers in the image domain, an improved watershed algorithm is proposed to segment the image into several local clusters of scattering centers. These clusters are decoupled and incorporated into a sparse representation-based parameter estimation process in the frequency domain, called Serialized Orthogonal Match Pursuit (SOMP). Thus, the algorithm complexity is reduced. Physically meaningful ASC parameter estimation results are obtained on synthesized data and simulated electromagnetic data. Meanwhile, a significant reduction in time and space complexity is achieved. An ASC parameter estimation dataset of the measured MSTAR dataset called “MSTAR-ASC” is open-sourced that can be used for downstream applications.
